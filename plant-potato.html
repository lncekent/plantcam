Heritage & Use

Originating in the Andes mountains of South America over 7,000 years ago, the potato was brought to Europe by Spanish conquistadors in the 16th century and became one of the world's most important staple crops. The Yukon Gold variety, developed in Canada in the 1960s, is prized for its golden flesh, buttery flavor, and versatility in the kitchen — perfect for boiling, mashing, roasting, and baking. It remains a cornerstone of global agriculture, feeding billions and sustaining entire economies.

Luminosity

Requires 6–8 hours of direct sunlight per day for optimal tuber development.

Hydration

Water consistently — 1–2 inches per week. Reduce after foliage yellows to avoid rot.

thermostat

Climate

Grows best in cool soil between 7°C and 18°C. Avoid frost after sprouting.

Disease Management

Late Blight

Water-soaked dark lesions on leaves and stems; caused by Phytophthora infestans.

health_and_safety Solution

Apply copper-based fungicide preventively; remove and destroy infected plants immediately.

Potato Scab

Rough, corky lesions on the tuber surface caused by Streptomyces scabies.

health_and_safety Solution

Maintain slightly acidic soil (pH 5.0–5.5) and rotate crops annually to break the cycle.
